Our Story

From virtual racing to a mission-driven motorsport team supporting cancer research

The Beginning

Montecito Racing was founded in Los Angeles, California by Andrew McDonald and Jack Berry, with a vision to build a competitive motorsport team. What began as a hobby, turned into a goal to become the intersection of racing excellence and charitable impact.

"At the beginning, I did not know exactly what Montecito Racing was going to be. The idea began as a virtual racing team built around competition and cars. Over time, as I reflected on the impact we could make, I saw the level of work being done at Cedars-Sinai, it became clear that this team should exist to support organizations like this through racing and fundraising."
— Andrew McDonald, Founder & Driver #88

Our Journey

2024 — The Start

From Idea to Reality

Andrew McDonald and Jack Berry meet through sim racing and begin building the foundation of what would become Montecito Racing. Initial focus on simulator-based endurance GT racing.

2024 — Mission Defined

Choosing Cedars-Sinai

The team commits to supporting cancer research at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center, establishing a clear charitable mission alongside competitive racing goals.

2025 — Present Day

Building Momentum

Continuing to expand the team, compete in simulator racing leagues, and raise awareness for Cedars-Sinai's cancer research programs. Building toward real-world GT4 endurance racing.

What's Next

Our vision extends beyond the virtual track.

We're building toward full participation on real tracks, with endurance GT4 racing as our long-term target. Every race, event, and piece of content is an opportunity to highlight Cedars-Sinai's work and invite people to support it. Our vision is a program where donors, fans, and patients feel connected to what happens on track.

Beyond competition, we use our platform to support cancer research at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center. What began as a virtual simulator racing team now has a clear purpose: connecting medical excellence with the excitement of racing.
